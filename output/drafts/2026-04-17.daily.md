## post 1

most people walk past 30 things every morning and notice none of them

magpie following me down the street

rain making the greens more saturated than usual

a raven just standing in a parking lot doing nothing

i notice all of it and it genuinely makes me better at my job

because design is just noticing

a coach's website that looks off but you can't explain why

a headline that's technically fine but something about it makes you scroll past

a layout that works in theory but kills trust in practice

you either see it or you don't

and the designers who don't notice things build websites that don't convert and can't tell you why

## post 2

## post 3

journaling about something beautiful makes the thing more beautiful

noticed that this morning watching magpies in the park

wrote about how the rain made everything more saturated and then actually stopped and looked at it properly

that's the whole mechanism

attention compounds

the more deliberately you notice something the more of it you see

i think about this with design constantly

most coaches glance at their website and think it's fine

they never actually sit with it the way a stranger would

thirty seconds on that page and a stranger has already decided whether to trust you

you get one shot at that first look and most people built their website without ever really looking at it

## post 5

a coach who can't explain why their website isn't converting is like a developer who can't explain why their code isn't working

the answer is in the details but you have to actually look

i helped someone figure this out recently

their website wasn't broken

the copy was decent

the offer was solid

but the page felt unserious

turns out it was a dozen small things

wrong font weight on the headline

hero image that looked like a stock photo from 2017

a testimonials section that looked like it was put there as an afterthought

none of those things alone kills you

all of them together tell the visitor this person is not the real deal

first impressions are not made by one thing

they're made by everything at once

and every detail either adds to the trust or subtracts from it

---

## post 6

nobody reads your about page the way you think they do

you wrote three paragraphs about your journey and your methodology and what sets you apart

your visitor read the first line and scanned the rest in two seconds

they were not looking for your story

they were looking for one thing

can i trust this person with my problem

that question gets answered by how the page LOOKS before a single word gets read

the photo quality

the layout

the spacing

whether it looks like someone who takes their business seriously built it or whether it looks like someone who figured canva was close enough

by the time they start reading you've already won or lost

most coaches are losing before their words even get a chance
