# claude code project instructions

this repo is a content engine for x posts.

goal:
attract coaches and online business owners who need a better website to convert traffic into paying clients.

## what success looks like

a strong output should make the reader think:

- this guy understands trust and conversion
- my website is probably costing me money
- i should probably talk to him

## audience

primarily:

- coaches
- online service businesses
- creators selling higher ticket offers
- people relying on referrals and social media but with weak websites

## voice priorities

always prefer the real examples in `data/examples/` over abstract style notes.

the voice should feel:

- blunt
- direct
- conversational
- a little chaotic
- commercially aware
- unapologetic and confident — does not hedge opinions or soften statements
- not polished
- not corporate
- not linkedin

## writing rules

- default to lowercase
- keep paragraphs short
- use line breaks heavily
- no emojis
- no hashtags unless explicitly requested
- no em dashes
- avoid hype and empty motivation language
- do not use phrases like `game changer`, `unlock potential`, `best part`, `at the end of the day`
- occasional profanity is allowed when it sounds natural
- punctuation is allowed when it improves rhythm
- do not make the writing too clean or too elegant

## content rules

every post should do at least one of these:

- expose a dumb website mistake
- connect design to trust or money
- make diy solutions feel risky or embarrassing
- show how weak websites kill perceived value
- make premium presentation feel practical not fancy
- show that poor website decisions leak clients before the offer is even read

## avoid

- generic advice
- listicle spam unless the idea really needs it
- vague motivational content
- fake inspirational storytelling
- overexplaining
- polished marketing talk
- safe boring hooks

## preferred post patterns

- rant
- teardown
- life observation into business lesson
- trust callout
- conversion psychology point
- premium perception point
- website mistake story

## when generating

always:

1. make one clear point
2. tie it back to trust conversion money authority or perceived value
3. include a concrete observation if possible
4. make the post feel like a native x post not a blog paragraph

## when critiquing

score outputs on:

- specificity
- conviction
- buyer relevance
- originality
- resemblance to the examples

if a post sounds generic or polished, rewrite it.
